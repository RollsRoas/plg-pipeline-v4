Lead-Intake Pipeline

Stages, dispositions & flow — an optimized intake design for PLG's TCPA practice, modeled for Monday and built to run ~1,000 qualified, signed cases a month.

Prepared forMario I. Molina, Esq.
DateJune 22, 2026
PlatformMonday
TypeRecommendation

An optimized intake pipeline for PLG's TCPA practice: every stage, the doors out of each stage (its dispositions), who owns it, and how long it should sit before someone acts.

Written to be read by a principal in ten minutes, and to double as the build spec for the board in Monday.

It is source-agnostic — it works whether a lead comes from PLG's own campaigns, a referral, a walk-in, or any vendor.

This is a working design and a suggestion; the final setup is PLG's call.

Why this design — what makes an intake pipeline effective

The target is throughput at scale: on the order of 1,000 qualified, signed cases a month moving cleanly from first contact to filed.

At that volume nothing can depend on a person remembering to check a lead. The board has to route, time, and escalate by itself.

An effective pipeline:

  • gives each stage one meaning, one owner, and one clock
  • leaves every lead with exactly one obvious next action
  • recycles a winnable-but-not-ready lead instead of discarding it
  • keeps origin, channel, evidence, and reason as fields — so the board stays short and reports slice any way
  • routes and escalates automatically, because no one reads 1,000 cards by hand

What it is built to avoid:

  • overloaded buckets where three different problems hide under one label
  • leads with no owner and no clock that quietly go cold
  • dead-ending a winnable lead as "disqualified"
  • inventing stages for things that are really attributes (time, channel, source)
  • manual tracking that collapses the moment volume climbs

How to read this

Four words carry the whole document:

  • Lead — a person. One record per claimant.
  • Case — one claim against one sender (defendant). A Lead opens one or more Cases.
  • Stage — where a Lead or Case is right now (a column on the board).
  • Disposition — a door out of a stage: where the record goes next, and on what condition.

Two boards, linked:

  • the Lead board runs the person from first contact to signed retainer
  • the Case board runs each claim from evidence to filed

Color is meaning. Every diagram uses the same seven categories, so a glance tells you what kind of state a box is:

BlackEntry — just arrived, untouched
BlueIn motion — actively being worked
GreenForward / win — advancing or won
AmberBottleneck — needs attention
SlateParked, recyclable — alive, will re-enter
TealParked, good claim / wrong geography — an asset on ice
RedClosed — dead

1 · The model: a person, and their claims

A TCPA claimant has often been hit by one or several senders — unwanted texts, prerecorded calls, or ringless voicemails.

Each sender is a separate case on its own schedule. One can be filed while another is still gathering evidence and a third is dead.

So the board separates the person (the Lead) from the claim (the Case):

After signing, the claim is the unit the board tracks, not the person.

A claimant who returns later with a new sender does not become a new Lead — a new Case is added under the Lead they already have.

2 · The Lead board — stages, owners, clocks, dispositions

Six stages run the person from arrival to signature.

Stages are checkpoints, not waiting rooms: most leads qualify and sign on the same call, crossing several stages in minutes. The board stamps each one as it passes, so the timing reports still work.

2.1 · New Lead

Owner: Setter · ⏱ Clock: first dial within 1 minute in business hours (alert at 5).

The logic: a lead rests here only while it is untouched. The first firm to respond is the one that signs, so this is the most aggressive clock on the board, and it runs only in business hours — an overnight arrival starts its clock when the desk opens.

2.2 · Attempting Contact

Owner: Setter · ⏱ Clock: ~6 attempts across ~7 days.

The logic: the setter is dialing but has not connected. Dispositions:

  • connected → Working / Qualifying
  • cadence exhausted, never reached → Nurture / Recovery (alive — worth re-engaging)
  • wrong or bad number → Disqualified

2.3 · Working / Qualifying

Owner: Setter (+ Intake QA) · ⏱ Clock: resolve on the same call.

The logic: with the claimant on the line, this stage answers two questions that decide everything after — is the lead in a covered state, and is there complete evidence for at least one case. Those two answers run the gate in §3. Dispositions:

  • passes the gate → sign on the call (most) or by link (the rest)
  • covered, evidence incomplete → Nurture / Recovery
  • not covered but has evidence → Coverage Hold (good claim, wrong geography)
  • no claim, or not covered and no evidence → Disqualified

2.4 · Retainer Sent — the exception path

Owner: Intake · ⏱ Clock: light nudge at ~2 h, day 1, day 3.

The logic: most leads sign live, so this stage holds only the ones who did not. The Adobe Sign link is out and short, and most people sign within minutes — so the follow-up is gentle and finite, never a barrage, so the lead is not burned. Dispositions:

  • signs → Retained
  • raises a concern → Objection / Follow-up
  • silent past day 5 → Nurture / Recovery (cold after retainer)

2.5 · Objection / Follow-up

Owner: Senior setter / Attorney · ⏱ Clock: 3–5 days, then defer.

The logic: coverage and evidence are fine, the client only hesitated on signing. Worked deliberately, not pestered. Dispositions:

  • resolved, signs → Retained
  • defer → Nurture / Recovery
  • declines → Disqualified

2.6 · Retained

Owner: Intake → Case team.

The logic: the retainer is executed and the board opens one Case per qualifying sender. The first case — already fully evidenced to pass the gate — opens directly at Complaint Drafting; any additional senders open at Evidence Pending (§5).

3 · The Send-Retainer Gate and the 2×2 fork

A retainer link goes out only when both are true:

  1. Coverage — the lead is in a state PLG serves.
  2. Evidence — there is a complete record for at least one case: the qualifying contacts for one sender (texts, calls, or voicemails) with timestamps and the sender line.

When both hold, the Adobe Sign link is sent — short, ready to sign — and most leads sign on the call. The rest sit briefly in Retainer Sent (§2.4).

The two questions produce four outcomes, and three of them are not "dead":

Complete evidence (≥1 case)No / partial evidence
In coverage Send the retainer → sign Nurture / Recovery — complete the evidence, then re-enter
Out of coverage Coverage Hold — evidenced claim parked for when PLG opens that state, or referred to co-counsel Disqualified — nothing to work and no coverage

Collecting one case's evidence before sending the retainer is what prevents the common failure mode: people who sign and then never produce a usable record.

4 · The three resting places — none of them is the trash

A Lead that is not moving forward lands in exactly one of three places, chosen by reason, not by accident.

Nurture / Recovery — recyclable, alive.
A claim probably exists, but something is missing today. Parked with a reason, worked on a short re-engagement cadence, and re-entered the moment the gap closes. Reasons and the play each one gets:

  • not ready → callback / drip
  • partial evidence (e.g. one of two messages) → SMS campaign to capture the second after-hours contact
  • no evidence yet → recovery-setter call
  • cold after retainer → spaced re-engage
  • never reached → recovery dialer / SMS

Coverage Hold — good claim, wrong geography.
An evidenced claim in a state PLG does not serve yet. Not dead — an asset on ice. It is either reactivated when PLG opens that state, or referred to co-counsel for a referral fee. Reviewed against the coverage roadmap on a set cadence.

Disqualified — dead.
No claim is possible and never will be: in-hours only, prior consent, non-marketing sender, a single contact that cannot be supplemented, suppressed repeat plaintiff, wrong/bad number, or out of coverage with no evidence. Closed with a reason; kept for suppression.

At volume this matters in dollars: the partial-evidence and "couldn't send it today" leads already told you they were contacted. They are among the most winnable cases on the board — recycling them is a second, cheaper source of signed retainers.

5 · The Case board — one track per sender

Eight stages run each claim from evidence to filed.

The first case enters at Complaint Drafting — its evidence already cleared the gate. Additional senders enter at Evidence Pending.

5.1 · Evidence Pending

Owner: Setter / Intake · ⏱ Clock: 14 days (nudge day 3 / 7 / 14).

The logic: gathering valid evidence for an additional sender, or for a recovered partial. The historical bottleneck — colored amber so it is always the thing being watched. Dispositions:

  • evidence received → Evidence Review
  • none producible → Disqualified

5.2 · Evidence Review

Owner: Intake QA / Paralegal · ⏱ Clock: 2 business days.

The logic: confirm the evidence actually qualifies — analyzer score, sender identity, after-hours timing, two or more contacts. Dispositions:

  • valid → Complaint Drafting
  • invalid but recoverable → back to Evidence Pending
  • invalid, terminal → Disqualified

5.3 · Complaint Drafting

Owner: Paralegal / Attorney · ⏱ Clock: 5 business days (pauses while held).

The logic: evidence is validated and the complaint is being written. Dispositions:

  • complete → Ready to File
  • needs one datum → Client Info Needed
  • firm pauses it → On Hold
  • defect cannot clear → Disqualified

5.4 · Client Info Needed

Owner: Setter / Intake · ⏱ Clock: 7 days.

The logic: the draft is blocked on one missing item — a mailing address, a confirmation. Dispositions:

  • datum obtained → Complaint Drafting
  • unreachable → Nurture / Recovery

5.5 · On Hold

Owner: Attorney · ⏱ Clock: 30-day review.

The logic: paused on purpose at the firm's direction — for example, pending a related sender's outcome. Alive, not stuck. Dispositions:

  • cleared → Complaint Drafting
  • firm drops it → Disqualified

5.6 · Ready to File → Filed → Resolved / Settlement

Owner: Filing attorney → Litigation · ⏱ Clock: file within 5 business days of Ready to File.

The logic: a cleared case waiting to be filed is stalled revenue, so a breach here escalates. Then the case is filed, then it closes on the merits.

6 · The whole picture, integrated

The gate is the hinge. The on-call sign is the main event. The two recovery loops on the left feed back in. Only Disqualified is final.

7 · Time governance — SLA and what happens when it runs out

Every stage has a default clock. The clocks are recommended starting points and can be adapted as volume and staffing change — the value is the action on breach, not the exact number.

At ~1,000 cases a month the escalation is staged and automatic, so a forgotten lead surfaces on its own:

  • at 80% of the clock → a private nudge to the owner
  • at the clock → the breach action below
  • past the clock → escalate and re-assign to a fallback queue

The setter supervisor receives every breach and escalation notification. All clocks run on business hours.

8 · Ownership and daily focus

Every stage has exactly one owner, so a lead or case is always somebody's next action. Owners are roles; PLG assigns its people.

RoleStages owned
Setter / IntakeNew Lead · Attempting Contact · Working / Qualifying · Retainer Sent · Evidence Pending · Client Info Needed
Recovery setterthe Nurture / Recovery lane
Senior setter / AttorneyObjection / Follow-up
Intake QAWorking / Qualifying (assist) · Evidence Review · all Disqualified (logs the reason)
Paralegal / AttorneyComplaint Drafting · On Hold · Ready to File
LitigationFiled · Resolved / Settlement
Setter supervisorevery critical and SLA-breach notification across the board

Worked first, every day:

  • New Lead — speed-to-lead; most leads sign on that first call, so this is where most revenue is won or lost.
  • Evidence Pending — the bottleneck for additional and recovered cases; clearing it turns retainers into filable claims.
  • Ready to File — a cleared case waiting to be filed is stalled revenue.

The Nurture / Recovery lane runs on its own cadence, reviewed weekly. Coverage Hold is reviewed against the coverage roadmap quarterly.

9 · The Lead's status is a roll-up of its cases

After Retained, the person's status is derived from their claims, not set by hand:

Lead statusRule
Activeat least one case is still moving (any stage before Filed).
Wonall cases closed and at least one reached Filed or Resolved.
Lostall cases Disqualified and none was ever filed.

A client with one filed case and one dead case reads Active / Won, never "disqualified."

10 · Fields — what travels on the card, not as a stage

On the Lead:

  • Source / Originated From — intake team · direct · referral · vendor
  • Coverage — state served (yes / no)
  • Channel — call · chat · SMS · email (the Adobe Sign link is the sign channel)
  • Language — EN · ES
  • Last Contact (date) · Recovery reason · Coverage-hold reason
  • Product — quiet-hours · stop / opt-out · bot-call / voicemail
  • Repeat-plaintiff flag · Geo (state) · Consent token (TrustedForm / timestamp)

On each Case:

  • Sender (defendant)
  • Disqualification reason

Disqualification reasons — one list, two origins:

  • Eligibility: no marketing intent · in-hours only · single contact only · sender unidentifiable / foreign · prior consent · mass-blast self-trail · repeat-plaintiff suppressed · out of coverage with no evidence · wrong / bad number
  • Case-level: no valid evidence · non-marketing sender · defendant not valid · no violation · missing information (unrecoverable) · client opt-out

11 · The board is run from views

A view is a saved filter, grouping, and sort — how the team works the board day to day.

Lead board views:

  • New — uncontacted (oldest first)
  • Working contact (Attempting + Working, by Last Contact)
  • Awaiting signature (Retainer Sent, by days-since-sent)
  • Objections
  • Recovery by reason (drives the re-engagement plays)
  • Coverage Hold (by state, against the roadmap)
  • Disqualified by reason · By source · By channel

Case board views:

  • Evidence queue (Evidence Pending, oldest first)
  • To validate (Evidence Review)
  • Blocked (Client Info Needed or On Hold)
  • Ready to file
  • Cases by claimant · Cases by sender

Every view also has an SLA-breach view — anything past its clock — which is what the setter supervisor watches.

12 · Key measures — read off the board, no invented metric

Lead:

  • time to first contact · contact rate · qualification rate
  • share signed on the call vs by link (a health signal — if the link share climbs, setters are not closing live)
  • signature rate (Retained ÷ Retainer Sent) · disqualification rate by reason

Recovery & coverage:

  • recovered-to-retained rate
  • share of signed retainers sourced from recovery (if 10%+, it is a real channel)
  • Coverage-Hold reactivations and referrals

Case:

  • cases filed per retained client · evidence cycle time · blocked count and age
  • filing rate (Filed ÷ opened) · time to file (Retained → Filed) · case disqualification rate by reason

By source / channel:

  • every measure sliceable on Source or Channel
  • cost per filed case by source

13 · Source-agnostic intake and the two-way hand-off

Raw inquiries from any source enter at New Lead.

Leads that arrive already signed with an evidence package — delivered by the intake team — enter at Retained and open their cases. Same board, no per-source stages.

The pre-signed contract runs both ways:

  • into Retained, the source guarantees, per case, a signed retainer plus the evidence package: screenshots or call records, timestamps, analyzer score, consent answers, product type
  • back to the source, a case that lands in Evidence Pending or Client Info Needed, or hits a defendant-validity defect, flags back — the source recovers the item or confirms the disqualification
  • the §10 disqualification list is shared, so a returned case carries a reason both sides report on

14 · Building it in Monday

The model maps onto Monday natively — parent items, subitems, status columns, people columns, saved views, and automations. No add-ons.

ConceptIn Monday
Leada parent item on the intake board
Casea subitem under that lead — one per sender
Lead stage (§2)a Status column on the item — the six lead stages, plus Nurture / Recovery, Coverage Hold, Disqualified
Case stage (§5)a Status column on the subitem — the eight case stages
Stage owner (§8)a People column on item and subitem
SLA + breach (§7)a date-stamp per move + an automation that nudges the owner at 80% and alerts the setter supervisor on breach
Coverage · Channel · Language · Source · Recovery reason · Product · Consentcolumns on the item
Sender · Disqualification reasoncolumns on the subitem
Re-entry from Recovery / Coverage Holdmove the item's status back to Working / Qualifying (an automation can trigger on a field flip)
Lead roll-up status (§9)a Mirror column over the subitems' stages, reduced to Active / Won / Lost by a Formula column
Views (§11)saved board views — Kanban by the stage column, Main Table grouped by a field, plus an SLA-breach view

Each stage maps to a Status column, each card to a People owner, each move to a dated SLA.

So at 1,000 cases a month a lead can never sit in a stage with no owner, no clock, and no next action — which is the whole point.
